Grace in the Margins

A space where God's presence meets life's detours

Begin

These writings were never created to fulfill a plan or advance an agenda.

Each piece was written in response to something lived, observed, read, or endured — often without any thought of where it might belong later. Over time, themes began to emerge, and the collections you see here were formed organically, after the fact.

This library is offered as it is, not as instruction, but as witness — in the hope that others might find encouragement, strength, or the quiet resolve to endure.

When life is falling apart

These readings are for those who find themselves repeating what they long to escape. They do not offer quick fixes or simple answers, but honest witness to grace that meets us in confinement—and begins release from within it.

When you’ve been hurt

These writings are for those carrying injuries they did not choose. They speak gently to harm, betrayal, and loss, without minimizing pain or rushing healing—holding space for restoration that unfolds in its own time.

When you have nothing left to give

These readings are for seasons of exhaustion, when faith feels thin and strength feels spent. They are offered as quiet companionship—reminding us that rest, renewal, and perseverance often arrive slowly, and together.

Learning to follow

Guided paths for first steps, hard seasons, and the steady work of walking with God over time.

Learning to listen

Readings for quieting noise, attending to Scripture, and responding to God’s voice with discernment and obedience.

Learning to know Him

Paths focused on God’s character, worship, and the kind of trust that grows through faithful practice.

Get to know the author

If you would like to understand the voice behind these writings, this path gathers pieces that reflect the journey, failures, questions, and grace that have shaped this work.
